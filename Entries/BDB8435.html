Biblical Hebrew

[שָׁבַץ] verb Pi`el probably weave in chequer or plaited work (Late Hebrew שָׁבַץ ornament a vessel with a pattern; Syriac (Lexicons) immiscuit, commixtio: see also LagBN 143); —

Pi`el Perfect 2 masculine singular וְשִׁבַּצְתָּ֫ Exod 28:39 ( P ), and thou shalt chequer (or plait) the tunic (in) byssus.
Pu`al Participle masculine plural מְשֻׁבָּצִים זָהָב Exod 28:20 ( P ), inwoven (i.e. set in a chequered or plaited mounting) with gold (-thread) shall they be (of gems).
