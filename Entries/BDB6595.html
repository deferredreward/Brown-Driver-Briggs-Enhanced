Biblical Hebrew

פָּטַר verb separate, remove, set free (Late Hebrew depart, set free, Niph`al also die; Phoenician פטר CIS102 a, 2 depart (die ?); Assyrian pa‰âru , split, break through, ip‰iru , ransom, Tel Amarna escape, set free (perhaps Canaanite); Arabic cleave, split, also create; Ethiopic create, fabricate; Aramaic פְּטַר, withdraw, depart); —

Qal Perfect 3 masculine singular ׳ פ 2Chr 23:8 Imperfect 3 masculine singular וַיִּפְטַר 1Sam 19:10 Participle active פּוֺטֵר Prov 17:14 passive פִּטוּרִים 1Chr 9:33 Qr ( Kt פטירים); construct פְּטוּרֵי 1Kgs 6:18; 1Kgs 6:29; 1Kgs 6:32, פְּטֻרֵי 1Kgs 6:35 —
1. intransitive remove oneself, specifically escape 1Sam 19:10 (מִפְּנֵי, compare Dr).
2. transitive set free from duty 2Chr 23:8 (accusative of person), so passive 1Chr 9:33 Qr ( Kt פטירים adjective or Aramaic participle, same meaning); יִם ׳ פ Prov 17:14 set free, let out; passive פְּטוּרֵי צִצִּים technical term of ornament in temple, dubious, usually out-spread (garlands) of flowers 1Kgs 6:18; 1Kgs 6:29; 1Kgs 6:32; 1Kgs 6:35.
