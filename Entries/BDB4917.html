[נאַף] verb commit adultery (Late Hebrew id.; Aramaic נְאַף (rare)): —

Qal Imperfect 3 masculine singular יִנְאַף Lev 20:10 (twice in verse); 3 masculine plural וַיִּנְאָ֑פוּ Jer 5:7 + 3 t. Imperfect; Infinitive absolute נָאוֺף Jer 23:14 נָאֹף Hos 4:2; Jer 7:9 Participle נֹאֵף Prov 6:32; Job 24:15 feminine singular נֹאָ֑פֶת Lev 20:10 feminine plural נֹאֲפוֺת Ezek 16:38 + 2 t.; —
1. literally commit adultery:
\ \ a. usually of man, always with wife of another; with accusative woman, Lev 20:10 (twice in verse) (H), Prov 6:32 elsewhere absolute Exod 20:14 = Deut 5:17 (Ten Words), Jer 5:7; Jer 7:9; Jer 23:14; Hos 4:2 participle masculine Lev 20:10 (H), Job 24:15.
\ \ b. of women, only participle Lev 20:10 (H), Ezek 16:38; Ezek 23:45 (twice in verse).
2. figurative of idolatrous worship, את האבן ואת העץ Jer 3:9. \ \ a. of man, with accusative woman, Jer 29:23 absolute Ezek 23:37 pt plural Jer 9:2; Jer 23:10; Ps 50:18.
