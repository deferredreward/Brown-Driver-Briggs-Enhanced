I. [סָלַל] verb lift up, cast up (Late Hebrew סִלְסֵל esteem highly, סִלְסוּל loftiness, distinction; סוּלָּם ladder; ᵑ7 סוּלְּמָא id.; MI26 מסלת highway; Phoenician סלמת stair (?) Lzb329; Assyrian sellu, sillu, perhaps breast-works DlHWB 501; Arabic is draw out, forth Lane1395 = שָׁלַל, but ladder (a loan-word according to SchwZMG liii (1899), 197), compare סֻלָּם; see also ‰rry, siege-wall, Egyptian loan-word from סֹלְלָה according to WMMAs.u.Eur.101); —

Qal Imperfect וַיָּסֹ֫לּוּ Job 19:12; Job 30:12 Imperative masculine plural סֹ֫לּוּ Isa 57:14 + 4 t.; suffix סָלּוּהָ Jer 50:26 (compare סֶ֫לָה); Passive participle סְלוּלָה Jer 18:15 סְלֻלָה Prov 15:19 —
1. cast up a highway: מְסִלָּה ׳ס Isa 62:10 (twice in verse), without object Isa 57:14 (twice in verse), דֶּרֶךְ לֹא סְלוּלָה Jer 18:15 figurative of path of upright Prov 15:19.
2. cast up a way: דֶּרֶךְ with על against, Job 19:12 (of besieger, in figure), ארה Job 30:12 (of besetting foe); Babylonian as a heap of garbage Jer 50:26.
3. lift up (a song) Ps 68:5, with לְ person (|| שׁיר, זמּר; most as
1.). Pilpel Imperative suffix סַלְסְלֶהָ Prov 4:8 exalt her (i.e. Wisdom), i.e. (compare Toy) esteem highly, prize. Hithpa`el Participle מִסְתּוֺלֵל Exod 9:17 (J) exalt oneself, with בְּ against (denominative from סֹלְלָה according to Gerber52); compare Eccl 39:24; Eccl 40:28.
