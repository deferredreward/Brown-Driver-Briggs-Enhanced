[פָּלַל]84 verb probably intervene, interpose (hence both arbitrate, judge , and intercede, pray, compare Dr 1Sam 2:25 ; Late Hebrew פלל (rare), נתפלל, pray, פִּילּוּל act of prayer, פְּלִילָה judicial matter; — otherwise WeHeid. (2), 126 who conjectures derivation for הִתְפַּלֵּל from Arabic notch edge (of sword, etc.), i.e. cut one-self in worship); —

Pi`el mediate, judge: Perfect 3 masculine singular consecutive, arbitrate, suffix וּפִלְלוֺ 1Sam 2:25 (but read probably 3 plural וּפִלְלוּ We Dr and others, word-play with ׳ יִתְפ following); 2 feminine singular פִּלַּלְתְּ Ezek 16:52 mediate for (ל person) namely, through thine own sins; 1 singular פִּלָּ֑לְתִּי Gen 48:11 ( E ; possibly denominative from פליל) I did not judge (have the opinion, expect) to see, etc.; Imperfect 3 masculine singular וַיְפַלֵּל Ps 106:30 (absolute) he interposed (by slaying offender).
Hithpa`el80 Perfect 3 masculine singular הִתְפַּלֵּל 1Kgs 8:42 1 singular הִתְפַּלָּ֑לְתִּי 1Sam 1:27, etc.; Imperfect יִתְפַּלֵּל Ps 32:6 +; יִתְפַּלֶּלֿ 1Sam 2:25 1 singular אֶתְאֶתְפּלָּ֑ל Ps 5:3, etc.; Imperative הִתְפַּלֵּל Num 21:7 +, etc.; Infinitive construct הִתְפַּלֵּל Ezek 10:1 +, etc.; Participle מִתְפַּלֵּל Jer 42:4 +, etc.; —

2. generally pray, with אֶל, unto idol Isa 44:17; Isa 45:20 unto Israel vIsa 45:14 ׳ בוֺא אלמֿקדשׁ להתפ Isa 16:12.
