Biblical Hebrew

טַבָּח noun masculine 1Sam 9:23

1. cook,
2. guardsman;
—
1. cook (who also killed the animal for food and served it) טַבָּח absolute 1Sam 9:23; 1Sam 9:24 .
2. elsewhere only plural טַבָּחִים guardsmen, bodyguard (originally royal slaughterers; see RSOTJC 426 (262); Semitic i. 1st ed., 396); always in the following combinations: ׳ שַׂר הַטּ captain of Pharaoh's bodyguard Gen 37:36; Gen 39:1 (both J ), Gen 40:3 ; Gen 40:4; Gen 41:10; Gen 41:12 (all E ); ׳ רַבטֿ chief of Nebuchadrezzar's bodyguard 2Kgs 25:8; 2Kgs 25:10; 2Kgs 25:11; 2Kgs 25:12; 2Kgs 25:15; 2Kgs 25:18; 2Kgs 25:20; Jer 39:9 + 16 t. Jeremiah (hence Aramaic רַב טַבָּחַיָּא Dan 2:14).
