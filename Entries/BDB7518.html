Biblical Hebrew

I. [קָצָה] verb cut off (Late Hebrew id., separate, Phoenician קצה cut off, exterminate; Aramaic קְצָא, break off (e.g. bread); Arabic be remote, remote extremity); —

Qal Infinitive קְצוֺת עַמִּים Hab 2:10 cutting off many peoples; Vrss קַצּוֺת (√ קצץ); Gr Now conjecture הֲצִיקוֺתָ.
Pi`el Infinitive construct לִקַצּוֺת בְּ 2Kgs 10:32 to cut off in Israel; read probably לִקְצוֺף to be angry with, ᵑ7 Hi CheCrit. n. Isa 14:6 (in Commentaries) KmpKau Kit Benz, ᵑ9 ; Gr לָקוּץ; Participle מְקַצֶּח רַגְלַיִם Prov 26:6 (figurative).
