מֶ֫תֶג noun masculine bridle (Aramaic ( ᵑ7 ) מִתְגָּא; Late Hebrew מֶתֶג of secondary accent ָֽ; מִתֵּג Pi`el denominative put on a bridle); —

1. literally bridle, for animals; מֶתֶג לַחֲמוֺר Prov 26:3 a whip for the horse, a bridle for the ass, etc.; וָרֶסֶן ׳ בְּמ Ps 32:9 with bridle and halter (on text of verse compare Che ); of ׳ י's birdle for Sennach. (under figure of beast), וּמִתְגִּי בִּשְׂפָתֶיךָ 2Kgs 19:28 and I will put (וְשַׂמְתִּי) my hook in thy nose and my bridle in thy lips = Isa 37:29 (see DrIs. ed. 2, 220).
2. figurative = control, authority, only in הָאַמָּה ׳ מ 2Sam 8:1 authority of the mother city, according to MV Buhl, of especially We Dr; Bu omit as corrupt (see I. אַמָּה , above); see, further, HPS.
