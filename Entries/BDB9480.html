ו

וְ, וּ conjunction and (Biblical Hebrew וְ), used mostly as in Hebrew, except that there is no ו 'consecutive'; — and, connecting both words (Dan 2:5) and sentences (ib. ). When three or more words are connected together, וְ may connect them all (Dan 2:6; Dan 2:10; Dan 4:34; Dan 5:18), or only the two last (Dan 2:17 ; Dan 3:4; Dan 4:4; Ezra 6:9), or the three last (Dan 2:37 ; Dan 3:21), or be omitted altogether (Dan 2:27; Dan 5:11, compare Dan 3:2; Ezra 4:9). וְ also connects verbs, in cases where Biblical Hebrew would use וַּ , as Dan 4:2; Dan 5:29; Dan 6:17; Dan 6:25 in such cases a Perfect is often followed by a Participle, as Dan 2:7 עֲנוֺוְאָֽמְרִין, so vDan 2:10; Dan 3:9; Dan 3:16; Dan 3:24 +, hence in Dan 2:5; Dan 2:8; Dan 2:15; Dan 2:20 and often read probably with NöGGA 1884, 1021 עֲנָה וְאָמַר (for עָנֵה). Special senses: —

a. and that Dan 4:10; Dan 4:12; Dan 4:20; Ezra 6:8; Ezra 6:9 (compare Dan 2:16; Dan 2:18); and also Dan 6:29 = with, Dan 7:1.
b. connecting contrasted ideas, but Dan 2:6; Dan 3:6; Dan 3:18; Dan 4:4.
c. = and so, Dan 2:49; Dan 6:2, especially after imperative Dan 2:4; Dan 2:9; Dan 2:24, or imperfect Dan 2:7.
d. to express an intention Dan 5:2 וְיִשְׁתּוֺן that they might drink: after a command, etc., even with participle or perf., nearly = that, Dan 2:13; Dan 6:2 (compare K§ 102).
e. introducing the predicate (compare Biblical Hebrew 5.c .), Dan 7:20 וְקִרְנָא דִכֵּן וְעֵינַיִם לַהּ and this horn, it had eyes; but the case is isolated, and del, probably וְ with Marti.
