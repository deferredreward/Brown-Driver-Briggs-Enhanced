[לָקַט] verb pick or gather up, specifically glean ( Late Hebrew id. and נָקַט; Arabic pick up (as a bird, grains, etc.); Aramaic לְקַט, and נְקַט ; ; Mandean לגט (NöM 54, also on Talmud נקט); compare Assyrian lakâtu DlHWB 385 (but ט = t?)); —

1. pick up, gather, with accusative, stones Gen 31:46 ( J ), lilies Song 6:2 usually the manna: Exod 16:4; Exod 16:5; Exod 16:26 ( J ), Exod 16:21 Exod 16:22 ( P ); followed by מִן partitive Exod 16:16 ( P ); so absolute Exod 16:27; Num 11:8 ( both J E ), Exod 16:17; Exod 16:18 ( P ); of animals gathering food תִּתֵּן לָהֶם יִלְקֹט֑וּן Ps 104:28 .
2. specifically glean, absolute, Ruth 2:8 (with בְּ local; usually Pi`el in this sense ).
