שָׁרַק verb hiss, perhaps also whistle, pipe (Late Hebrew id., hiss (dubious), Aramaic שְׁרֵיק id. ᵑ7 Lev 2:15; Lev 2:16, id.; — from ׳שׁ comes Greek σῦριγξ according to LagAbh GGW xxvi. 38 LewyFremdw.165); —

Qal Perfect 3 masculine singular consecutive ׳וְשׁ Isa 5:26, etc.; Imperfect 3 masculine singular יִשְׁרֹק Isa 7:18 + (Job 27:23 read יִשְׁרְקוּ Me Bi Siegf Bu); 1 singular אֶשְׁרְקָה Zech 10:8 — hiss, as signal, ל person, figurative, ׳י subject: Isa 5:26; Isa 7:18; Zech 10:1 in derision, עַל civitat., etc. (often + שָׁמֵם be astounded), Jer 19:8; Jer 49:17; Jer 50:13; Lam 2:15 (+ הֵנִיעַ ראֹשׁ), Ezek 27:36 absolute Zeph 2:15; Lam 2:16; 1Kgs 9:8 (+ חָרַק שֵׁן); עַל person Job 27:23 (with מִן local).
