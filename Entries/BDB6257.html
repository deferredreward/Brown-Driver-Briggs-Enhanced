BDB6256 | BIBLICAL HEBREW | BDB6258

עָפָר noun masculine Lev 14:42 dry earth, dust; — absolute ׳ ע Gen 2:7 +, construct עֲפַר Gen 13:16 +; suffix עֲפָרֵךְ Ezek 26:12, עֲפָרוֺ Deut 9:21, etc.; plural construct עַפְרֹת Job 28:6; Prov 8:26 —

1. literally:
a. dry, loose earth, thrown (in malice) 2Sam 16:13 (|| אֲבָנִים); עַלרֿאֹשׁ ׳ וַיַעֲלוּ ע Josh 7:6 ( J E ; sign of grief), so Ezek 27:30; Lam 2:10, and (with זָרַק) Job 2:12 loose earth (on surface of ground) 1Kgs 18:38; Amos 2:7; Mic 1:10; Isa 34:7; Isa 34:9; Lev 17:13 ( H ) Ezek 24:7 (in figurative), Num 5:17; Job 14:19; Job 38:38; Job 39:14; Gen 26:15 ( R ), made into siege works Hab 1:10 of sand-storm Deut 28:24 (+ אָבָק); as serpent's food, token of punishment and humiliation, Gen 3:14 ( J ) Isa 65:25 (compare Mic 7:17 2.e. below); ׳ זֹחֲלֵי ע Deut 32:24 (poem; compare 2.e.).
b. specifically as material of human body Gen 2:7 ; Gen 3:19 ( J ), Eccl 3:20, compare Job 4:19; Job 8:19; Ps 103:14, to which it returns (שׁוּב אֶלֿ) Gen 3:19; Job 10:9; Ps 104:29; Eccl 3:20 so (with עַל) Job 34:15; Eccl 12:7.
c. = surface of ground Exod 8:12 ; Exod 8:13 (twice in verse); ( P ; הָאָרַץ ׳ ע), Job 19:25; Isa 25:12; Isa 26:5 (both || אֶרֶץ), surface of (whole) earth Job 41:25, ׳ מְחִלוֺת ע Isa 2:19 (for hiding), so ׳ ע alone Isa 2:10, compare ׳ חֹרֵי ע Job 30:6 = soil, Job 5:6; Job 14:8.
d. powder of anything pulverized Deut 9:21 (twice in verse) (compare דָּ֑ק || Exod 32:20), 2Kgs 23:6 (twice in verse); 2Kgs 23:12; 2Kgs 23:15, so in simile 2Sam 22:43 = Ps 18:43, hence = ashes v Ps 18:4; Num 19:17.
e. debris, of ruined city 1Kgs 20:10; Ezek 26:4; Ezek 26:12; Neh 3:34 ; Neh 4:4 ; Ps 102:15. The following are late:
f. earth of the grave: אֶשְׁכַּב ׳ לֶע Job 7:21 , so (with עַל) Job 20:11; Job 21:26, compare Job 17:16 ׳ יוֺרְדֵי ע Ps 22:30 , ׳ שֹׁכְנֵי ע Isa 26:19, ׳ יְשֵׁנֵי אַדְמַתעֿ Dan 12:2 עֲפַרמָֿוֶת Ps 22:16 ׳ ע in figure Ps 30:10.
g. mortar (i.e. dried mud) for plastering houses Lev 14:41; Lev 14:42; Lev 14:45 ( P ).
h. (iron-) ore Job 28:2 עַפְרֹת זָהָב Job 28:6 gold-dust (but see סַפִּיר ).
i. material of earth Isa 40:12 , compare ראֹשׁ עַפְרֹת תֵּבֵל Prov 8:26 the first of the earth particles of the world.
2. figurative:
a. of abundance Gen 13:16 (twice in verse); Gen 28:14 (all J ) 2Chr 1:9 ; Job 27:16; Ps 78:27 (|| חוֺל יַמִּים), compare עֲפַר יַעֲקֹב Num 23:10 ( J E ); with added idea of commonness, worthlessness Zeph 1:7; Zech 9:3.
b. of worthlessness (alone) Job 22:24.
c. simile of the scattered, dispersed 2Kgs 13:7 ׳ כֶּע Isa 41:2 (so van d. H. Ginsb; Baer ׳ כֵּע; on meaning see Du Che, reading תְּשִׂימֵם for יִתֵּן).
d. of self-abasement Gen 18:27 (+ אֵפַר), ׳ וְנִחַמְתִּי עַלעֿ וָאֵפֶר Job 42:6, פִּיהוּ ׳ יִתֵּן בֶּע Lam 3:29.
e. of humiliation (sitting or lying in dust) Isa 47:1 compare Isa 29:4 (twice in verse), Job 16:15; Job 40:13; Ps 7:6; Ps 44:26; Ps 119:25 , simile Job 30:19 licking dust Ps 72:9 ; Isa 49:23 ; Mic 7:17 , compare Tel Amarna tikalu ipra (of enemies, see WklAltor. Forsch. iii. 291 ; also of serpent 1.a. above); of ׳ י raising from the dust 1Sam 2:8 = Ps 113:7, 1Kgs 16:2 of lifting oneself Isa 52:2.
