[חָמַם] verb be or become warm (Late Hebrew id., Pi`el, make warm , Aramaic , חֲמַם be warm; Arabic become hot, of water; also heat, kindle fire in) —

Qal Perfect חַם Ps 39:4; Exod 16:21 וְחַם consecutive 1Kgs 1:2; Eccl 4:11 חַמּוֺתִ֫י Isa 44:16 Imperfect יָחֹם Isa 44:16 וַיָּ֫חָם 2Kgs 4:34; Isa 44:15 also יִחַם 1Kgs 1:1 יֵחַם Deut 19:6 יֵחָ֑ם Eccl 4:11 3 feminine singular תֵּחַם Ezek 24:11 יֵחַמּוּ Hos 7:7 וַיֶּחֱמוּ Gen 30:39 וַיֵּחַמְנָה Gen 30:38 (last seven forms could be also from יחם q. v.; but no decisive evidence that this √ is used in Qal; they are placed under חמם by Thes Ew§ 193 a Ol§ 243 b; see also Köi. 365, 417 ff., who on account of יחם Pi`el Infinitive Gen 30:41; Gen 31:10, derives last two, needlessly, from יחם); Infinitive לַחְמָם Isa 47:14, compare Job 30:4 (where, however, ׳ ל is from לֶחֶם Mich Di unless (Luzz Che) we read לְחַמֵּם Pi`el Infinitive in both); חֹם 1Sam 11:9; Hag 1:6; Neh 7:3 suffix חֻמּוֺ Job 6:17 חֻמָּם Jer 51:39 —
1. literally, be or grow warm Exod 16:21 ( P ; subject שֶׁמֶשׁ), compare 1Sam 11:9; Neh 7:3 and Job 6:17 (suffix of impersonal subject); from fire Isa 44:15; Isa 44:16 (twice in verse); Isa 47:14 (compare above); natural heat 1Kgs 1:1; 1Kgs 1:2 (by personal contact), so 2Kgs 4:34; Eccl 4:11 (twice in verse), from clothing Hag 1:6 of Jerusalem under figure of pot or caldron Ezek 24:11.
2. figurative, subject לִבִּי Ps 39:4 (|| בַהֲגִיגִי תִבְעַראֵֿשׁ), compare Deut 19:6 of conspirators Hos 7:7 Chaldeans Jer 51:39.
3. of heat in conception (animals) Gen 30:38; Gen 30:39 (compare יחם).
