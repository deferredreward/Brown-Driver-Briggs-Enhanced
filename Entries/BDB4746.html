BDB4745 | BIBLICAL HEBREW | BDB4747

מִסַּת noun sufficiency (derivation obscure: Aramaic מִיסַּת, , common in ᵑ7 ᵑ6 , both absolute and construct in sense of sufficiency, enough; e.g. for Hebrew רַב 2Sam 24:16 ᵑ7 , דַּי Exod 36:7 ᵑ7 ᵑ6 , Obad 1:5 ᵑ7 ᵑ6 , for לְפִי Exod 12:4 ᵑ7 ; the amount of the debt, PS2183 ); — Deut 16:10 and thou shalt keep the pilgrimage of weeks מִסַּת נִדְבַת יָָֽדְךָ according to (adverb accusative) the sufficiency of the free-will offering of thy hand, which thou shalt give, i.e. with the full amount that thou canst afford (compare דֵּי שֶׂשׁה Lev 5:7; Lev 12:8 ᵑ7 כְּמִיסַּת אִימְּרָא).
