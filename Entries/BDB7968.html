רָמַס verb trample (Late Hebrew id.; Jewish-Aramaic רְמַס; compare perhaps Syriac , Arabic , kick (BaES 33 Köii. 196); is bury, and conceal grave); —

Qal Perfect 3 masculine singular consecutive ׳ וְר Mic 5:7 Imperfect 3 masculine singular יִרְמֹס Ezek 26:11; Ps 7:6, יִרְמָסֿ Isa 41:25, suffix וַיִּרְמְסֶנָּה 2Kgs 9:33, etc.; Imperative feminine singular רִמְסִי Nah 3:14 Infinitive construct רְמֹס Isa 1:12 Participle רֹמֵס Isa 16:4 — trample, of men, with accusative חֲצֵרָ֑י Isa 1:12, חוּצוֺת Ezek 26:11 (by horses' hoofs), טִיט Ezek 41:25 (in simile), compare בַחֹמֶר ׳ ר Nah 3:14, accusative מִרְעֶה Ezek 34:18 (in figurative); trample down, accusative of person, animal, or thing, 2Kgs 7:17; 2Kgs 7:20, so (by horses' hoofs) 2Kgs 9:33, לָאָרֶץ חַיָּי֫ ׳ יִר Ps 7:6; Ps 91:13 (|| תִּדְרֹךְ); subject ׳ י Isa 63:13 (figurative; +דָּרַךְ), subject רֶנֶל Isa 26:6 subject beast 2Kgs 14:9 (allegory) = 2Chr 25:18; Dan 8:7 (vision), of horn, vDan 8:10 absolute of lion, וְטָרַף ׳ וְר Mic 5:7 (simile); participle as substantive collective tramplers (i.e. devastators) Isa 16:4.
Niph`al be trampled, Imperfect 3 feminine plural בְּרַגֶלַיִם תֵּרָמַ֑סְנָה Isa 28:3 (on subject compare Di Du CheHpt Köi. 183).
