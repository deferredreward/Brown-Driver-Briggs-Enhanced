Biblical Hebrew

I. נֹ֫גַהּ noun feminine Hab 3:4 brightness; — ׳ נ absolute Amos 5:10 +; construct Isa 4:5 +; suffix נָגְהָם Joel 2:10; Joel 4:15 — brightness of a (clear, sunshiny) day Amos 5:10 (|| אוֺר; opposed to אָפֵל , חשֶׁךְ), 2Sam 23:4 (מִמָּטָר after rain); so figurative of Zion's dawning, זַרְחֵךְ ׳ נ Isa 60:3 (|| אוֺר); of her righteousness כַּנֹּגַהּ Isa 62:1 (with article only here and Ezek 1:28), || כְּלַפִּיד יִבְעָ֑ר; of moon Isa 60:19, of stars Joel 2:10; Joel 4:15 of theophany 2Sam 22:13 = Ps 18:13 (in storm); compare Ezek 1:4 ; Ezek 1:27 ; Ezek 1:28, ׳ כְּבוֺד י ׳ נ Ezek 10:4 כאור ׳ נ Hab 3:4 לָאֵשׁ ׳ נ Ezek 1:13, אֵשׁ לֶהָבָה ׳ נ Isa 4:5 בְּרַק ׳ נ חֲנִיתֶ֑ךָ Hab 3:11 (of glitter of ׳ י's spear; || אוֺר חִצֶּיךָ); of individual, לוֺ ׳ וְאֵין נ Isa 50:10 (figurative of distress || חֲשֵׁכִים); so ׳ וְאֹרַח צַדִּיקִים כְּאוֺר נ Prov 4:18 the path of righteous men is like a light of brightness.
