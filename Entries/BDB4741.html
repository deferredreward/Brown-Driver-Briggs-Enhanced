[מָסַס] verb dissolve, melt, intransitive ( ᵑ7 מְסַס; compare מסה, and II. מאס); —

Qal. Infinitive construct מְסֹס נֹסֵס Isa 10:18 like the melting (wasting away) of a נֹסֵס (q. v.)
Niph`al Perfect וְנָמֵס consecutive Ezek 21:12 וְנָמָ֑ס Exod 16:21; Ps 112:10 נָמַ֫סּוּ Ps 97:5 + 2 t; Imperfect יִמַּס Deut 20:8 +; וַיִּמַּסּוּ Judg 15:14 Infinitive absolute הִמֵּס 2Sam 17:10 construct id. Ps 68:3 Participle נָמֵס Ps 22:15 + 2 t.; —
1. melt (intransitive) of manna Exod 16:21 wax Ps 68:3 (simile of wicked); manacles Judg 15:14 (= vanished, dropped off); wicked Ps 112:10 (melt away ); especially of hills and mountains Mic 1:4; Ps 97:5; Isa 34:3.
2. most often figurative of heart = faint, grow fearful, Nah 2:11; 2Sam 17:10 (twice in verse) (compare Dr), Deut 20:8; Josh 2:11; Josh 5:1; Josh 7:5; Isa 13:7; Isa 19:1; Ezek 21:12; Ps 22:15.
3. Participle = wasted, consumptive (?), 1Sam 15:9 (|| נְמִבְזָה, read נִבְזָה see בזה Niph`al) < read נִמְאֶסֶת worthless, literally rejected (see מאס Niph`al)
Hiph`il Perfect 3 plural הֵמַסּוּ אֶתלְֿבָבֵנוּ Deut 1:28 cause to melt (= intimidate).
