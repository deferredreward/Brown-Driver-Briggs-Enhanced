I. [קִיץ] verb

Hiph`il awake (|| form of יקץ; Late Hebrew in Hiph`il (rare)); — Perfect 3 masculine singular הֵקִיץ 2Kgs 4:31; Ezek 7:6 2 masculine singular וַהֲקיצ֫וֺתָ consecutive Prov 6:22, etc.; Imperfect 1 singular אָקִיץ Prov 23:35 3 masculine plural יָקִ֫יצוּ Jer 51:39 +; Imperative masculine singular הָקִ֫יצָה Hab 2:19 +; masculine plural הָקִ֫יצוּ Isa 26:19; Joel 1:5 Infinitive construct הָקִיץ Ps 17:15; Ps 73:20 Participle מֵקִיץ 1Sam 26:12 — shew signs of waking, awake:
1. from sleep,
a. 1Sam 26:12 ; Isa 29:8 (twice in verse); Ps 3:6 ; Ps 73:20 ; Ps 139:18; Prov 6:22 (doubtful line, see Toy).
b. of ׳ י Ps 44:24 awake [from sleep] to activity, so Ps 35:23 (with ל of thing), Ps 59:6 (c. infinitive purpose).
c. from ecstatic sleep of prophets Jer 31:26.
2. from sleep of death, with negative 2Kgs 4:31; Jer 51:39; Jer 51:57; Job 14:12 of resurrection Isa 26:19; Dan 12:2
3. from stupor (of drunkenness) Joel 1:5; Prov 23:35.
4. of inanimate thing Hab 2:19 compare בָּא הַקֵּץ הֵקִיץ אֵלָ֑יִח Ezek 7:6 the end cometh, it hath awaked unto thee (Co strike out הֵקִיץ, but the word-play favours it). — Ps 17:15 is put by Thes Ol Hup Du and others below 1. a.; Ew De Che below 2; we below 1 b.; Calv Now from night of distress and helplessness, and so Bae (psalmist representing Israel).
