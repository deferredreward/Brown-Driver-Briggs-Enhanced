דֶּ֫שֶׁא noun masculine Isa 15:6 grass (Late Hebrew id., Assyrian dišu , herb, LyonSargontexte 69 ; Sabean דתֿא fresh shoots DHMZMG 1875, 597 = springtime CISiv. p. 11; Aramaic דִּתְאָה, (compare LagBN 130)) — ׳ ד absolute Gen 1:11+ 13 t. — accusative of cognate meaning with verb תַּדְשֵׁא Gen 1:11 compare v Gen 1:12 (in both, produced by earth); springing out of earth 2Sam 23:4 of a second crop of grass Prov 21:25 (opposed to חָצִיר); caused to spring forth by God לְהַצְמִיחַ מֹצָא דֶ֑שֶׁא Job 38:27 refreshed by rain Deut 32:2 ׳ נְאוֺת ד Ps 23:2 as food of wild ass Job 6:5 as failing (withered) Isa 15:6 lacking for animals Jer 14:5 וִירַק דֶּשֶׁא simile of weakness 2Kgs 19:26 (|| עֵשֶׂב שָׂדֶה) = Isa 37:27 of transitoriness (withering) Ps 37:2 (׳ יֶרֶק ד); of growth and prosperity, Isa 66:14.
