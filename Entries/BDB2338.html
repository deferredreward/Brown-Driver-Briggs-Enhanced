זֵד adjective insolent, presumptuous: — ׳ ז Prov 21:24 plural זֵדִים Jer 43:2 + 11 t.; — always masculine, and always of opposition to ׳ י, wickedness; with qualified noun expressed, only הָאֲנָשִׁים הַזֵּדִים Jer 43:2, of opponents of Jeremiah; elsewhere used substantively, as technical term for godless, rebellious men; once singular זֵד יָהִיר לֵץ שְׁמוֺ Prov 21:24 a haughty insolent one, scorner is his name (|| עוֺשֶׂה בְּעֶבְרַת זָדוֺן); usually plural Isa 13:11(׳ גְּאוֺן ז; || גַּאֲוַת עָרִיצִים); Mal 3:13 (|| עשֵֹׁי רִשְׁעָה), v. Mal 4:19 (|| רִשְׁעָה עשֵֹׁה); and in Ps 86:14 (|| עָרִיצִים), Ps 119:21(|| מִמִּצְוֺתֶיךָ הַשֹּׁגִים ), Ps 119:51 Ps 119:69 Ps 119:78 Ps 119:85 Ps 119:122 so probably also Ps 19:14 גַּם מִזֵּדִים חֲשׂךְ עַבְדֶּ֑ךָ also from presumptuous men hold back thy servant (Ew Ol Hup Che RVm ); > presumptuous sins Deal. ( AV RV ).
