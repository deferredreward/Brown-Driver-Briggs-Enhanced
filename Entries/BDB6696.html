II. [פָּסַח] verb limp (Thes below I. ׳פ but dubious; Arabic is dislocate; according to Gerber73 II. ׳פ is denominative from פִּסֵּחַ [and this below I. ׳פ?]); —

Qal Participle plural פֹּסְחִים עַלשְֿׁתֵּי הַסְּעַפִּים 1Kgs 18:21 figurative (see [ סְעִפָּה]). Niph`al Imperfect 3 masculine singular וַיִּפֹּל וַיִּפָּסֵחַ 2Sam 4:4 and he fell and was made limping (lame). Pi`el Imperfect 3 masculine plural וַיְפַסְּחוּ עַלהַֿמִּזְבֵּחַ 1Kgs 18:26 they went limping (along by the altar), i.e. probably in their dance, see Kit PietschmPhön. 220 (Gr ויפסעו [ = וַיִּפְשְׂעוּ] stepped).
