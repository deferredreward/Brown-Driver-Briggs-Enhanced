BDB4993 | BIBLICAL HEBREW | BDB4995

נִדָּה noun feminine impurity (as abhorrent, shunned); — absolute ׳ נ Ezek 18:6 +; construct נִדַּת Lev 12:2 +; suffix נִדָּתָהּ Lev 12:5 +; — impurity (especially P and Ezekiel):

1. of ceremonial impurity, as union with brother's wife, Lev 20:21 especially of menstruation Ezek 18:6 (read אִשָּׁה בְּנִדָּתָהּ for ᵑ0 נִדָּה ׳ א ᵑ6 Co), ׳ טְמֵאַת הַנּ Ezek 22:10, compare Ezek 36:17 , נִדַּת דְּוֺתָהּ Lev 12:2, compare v Lev 12:5; Lev 15:19; Lev 15:20; Lev 15:24; Lev 15:25 ( ׳ עֶתנִֿדּ), (3 t. in verse), Lev 15:26 (׳ מִשְׁכַּב נִדּ), Lev 15:26 (׳ טֻמְאַת נִדּ), Lev 15:33; Lev 18:19 of defilement contracted by contact with a corpse, מֵי (הַ)נִּדָּה water of impurity (i.e. to remove it, compare Di) Num 19:9; Num 19:13; Num 19:20; Num 19:21 (twice in verse); Num 31:23 .
2. figurative (with allusion to cerem. usage), impure thing, Ezek 7:19; Ezek 7:20 (of gold), Lam 1:17 (of Jerusalem); of idolatry, immorality, etc., 2Chr 29:5; Ezra 9:11 (אֶרֶץ נִדָּה), Ezra 9:11, Zech 13:1 (לְחַטַּאת וּלְנִדָּה).
