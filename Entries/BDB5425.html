BDB5424 | BIBLICAL HEBREW | BDB5426

נֶ֫שֶׁף noun masculine twilight (properly twilight-breeze, compare לְרוּחַ הַיּוֺם Gen 3:8 Late Hebrew id.; ᵑ7 נְשֶׁף, נִשְׁפָּא; — ׳נ absolute 1Sam 30:17 +, נָ֑שֶׁף Jer 13:16 construct Isa 21:4 suffix נִשְׁפּוֺ Job 3:9 —

1. evening twilight 2Kgs 7:5; 2Kgs 7:7 (compare לַיְלָה v2Kgs 7:12); opposed to בֹּקֶר Isa 5:11 time of concealment Job 24:15; Prov 7:9 (+ עֶרֶב יוֺם, אִישׁוֺן לַיְלָה וַאֲפֵלָה, to emphasize sin that shuns the day); of refreshing Isa 21:4 חִשְׁקִיׅ ׳(נ; of stumbling, in dim light Isa 59:10 (opposed to צָהֳרַיִם), so הָרֵי נָ֑שֶׁף Jer 13:16 (both figurative); כּוֺכְבֵי נִשְׁפּוֺ Job 3:9 the stars of its twilight (i.e. of night of Job's birth). — so וְעַדהָֿעֶרֶב ׳מֵהַנּ לְמָחֳרָתָם 1Sam 30 Klo Kit HPS (read לְהַחֲרִמָם for ׳לְמָח, We Kit Bu); Th, after Luth, below so BuhlKex SS; Löhr allows either.
2. morning twilight Job 7:4 (opposed to עֶרֶב). apparently also Ps 119:147.
