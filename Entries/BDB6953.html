BDB6952 | BIBLICAL HEBREW | BDB6954

I. [צֹ֫הַר] [noun masculine] only plural צָהֳרַ֫יִם midday, noon, Deut 28:29+, צָהֳרָ֑יִם Gen 43:16 +(when sun mounts its highest; on form as expanded plural (not dual) see Ges«GKC:88c»§ 88c and references); — usually

1. noon as a specific time of day, 1Kgs 18:29 especially ׳ בַּצּ at noon Gen 43:16; Gen 43:25; Amos 8:9; 1Kgs 18:27 ; 1Kgs 20:16 ; Song 1:7 (resting-time for flock), Jer 6:4 (opposed to evening); ׳ בְּעֵת צ Jer 20:16 (distinguished from morning) as time of supposed security Jer 15:8; Zeph 2:4 also ׳ מֵהַבֹּקֶר וְעַד הַצּ 1Kgs 18:26 , ׳ עַדהַֿצּ 2Kgs 4:20 without ב, as adverb, ׳ צ as time of prayer Ps 55:18 (+בֹּקֶר, עֶרֶב); as time of wasting קֶטֶב Ps 91:6 ׳ מִשְׁכַּב הַצּ 2Sam 4:5 noonday repose.
2. noon, as bright, simile of happiness, blessing, Isa 58:10 (opposed to אֲפֵלָה ), Ps 37:6 (|| כָּאוֺר); compare Job 11:17 (Ges«GKC:133e»§ 133e); see also ׳ בְּתוֺךְ הַצּ Isa 16:3 (opposed to צִלֵּךְ); ׳ בַּצּ Deut 28:29; Isa 59:10; Job 5:14.
