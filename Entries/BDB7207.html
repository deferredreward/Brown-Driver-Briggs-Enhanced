[צַפַף] verb

Pilpel chirp, peep (onomatopoetic; Late Hebrew Pilpal id.; Jewish-Aramaic צַפְצֵף chirp; Arabic sparrow, etc.); — Imperfect 3 feminine singular תְּצַפְצֵף Isa 29:4, 1 singular אֲצַפְצֵף Isa 38:14 Participle מְצַפְצֵף Isa 10:14, plural הַמְפְצְפִים Isa 8:19 — chirp, peep:
1. of birds Isa 10:14 (figurative of conquered peoples); of mourning, ׳ בְּסוּסאֲ֗֗֗צ Isa 38:14 (|| אֶהְנֶּה בַּיּוֺנָה).
2. of spirits, ghosts Isa 8:19 (+ הַמַּהְגִּים), ׳ מֵעָפָר אִמְרָתֵךְ תְּצ Isa 29:4 (like a spirit's).
