רָבַץ verb stretch oneself out, lie down, lie stretched out (Late Hebrew = Biblical Hebrew; Assyrian rabâƒu, lie, dwell; rubƒu (in word-lists), stall, also womb (compare II. רבע above); Arabic lie down, on the breast, stretch oneself out, Sabean מרבצֿן CISiv. no. 5, 2; ᵑ7 רְבַע lie stretched out, Syriac , Christian-Palestinian Aramaic recline at meals, SchwIdioticon 87); —

Qal Perfect 3 masculine singular ׳ר Gen 49:9, 3 feminine singular consecutive וְרָֽבְצָה Deut 29:19, רָבָ֑צָה Ezek 19:2, etc.; Imperfect 3 masculine singular יִרְבָּ֑ץ Isa 11:6; Isa 27:10 3 feminine singular וַתִּרְבַּץ Num 22:27 3 masculine plural יִרְבְּצוּ Num 11:7 יִרְבָּ֑צוּ Num 14:30 יִרְבָּצ֑וּן Zeph 2:7; Ps 104:22 3 feminine plural תִּרְבַּצְנָה Ezek 34:14 Participle רֹבֵץ Exod 23:5 + 3 t.; feminine רֹבֶ֫צֶת Gen 49:25 + 2 t.; masculine plural רֹבְצֹים Gen 29:2 — lie down, lie: of domestic animals, ass Exod 23:5 (E; under heavy burden), Num 22:27 (Jeremiah; in obstructed path), Gen 49:14 (at ease; poetic, in simile); sheep, in repose, Gen 29:2 (J), Isa 17:2; Zeph 2:14 figurative of people Ezek 34:14 calf. in repose Isa 27:10: of wild beasts, lion, in lair, Gen 49:9 (poem in J; figurative), Ps 104:22 = make lair, abode Ezek 19:2 (figurative), so of צִיִּים Isa 13:21, תַּנֵין Ezek 29:3 (figurative); leopard (with kid) Isa 11:6, compare vIsa 11:7 = brood, of mother-bird Deut 22:6 (עַלהָֿאֶפְרֹחִים); of man, in repose Isa 14:30; Job 11:19, compare Zeph 2:7; Zeph 3:13 (figurative of flock perhaps implied, compare Ezek 34:14 above); of the deep, רֹבֶצֶת תָּ֑חַת Gen 49:25; Deut 33:13 figurative of curse Deut 29:19 (ב person); of sin, לַפֶּתַח חַטָאת רֹבֵץ Gen 4:7 at the door sin makes its lair.
