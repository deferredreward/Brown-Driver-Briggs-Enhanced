BDB3367 | BIBLICAL HEBREW | BDB3369

טִפְסָר noun [masculine] scribe, marshal (if meaning correct, then Assyrian loan-word, from dupsarru, tablet-writer, scribe, see LenLangue Primit. de la Chaldeve, 365 SchrCOT on Jer 51:27, LotzTP 180; dupšarru , according to DlHWB 227) — plural suffix טַפְסְרַיִךְ Nah 3:17 (as if from טַפְסֵר) thy scribes, marshals, of high officials of Nineveh; absolute singular פִּקְדוּ עָלֶיהָ טִפְסָר Jer 51:27 appoint a marshal against her, i.e. against Babylon. — On military function of those skilled in writing compare שֹׁטֵר.
