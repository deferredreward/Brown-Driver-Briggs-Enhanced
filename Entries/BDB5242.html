BDB5242 [H5251 H3071]

BDB5241 | BIBLICAL HEBREW | BDB5243

נֵס noun [masculine] standard, ensign, signal, sign (Late Hebrew id., flag, usually sign, i.e. miracle; ᵑ7 נֵס, נִסָּא sign, miracle); — ׳ נ absolute Num 21:8 +; construct Isa 11:10 suffix נִסִּי Exod 17:15; Isa 49:22 —

1.
a. standard, as rallying-point, נִסִּי ׳ י Exod 17:15 ( E ), ׳ י my standard (name of altar); conspicuously set on hill Isa 30:17 (simile of solitariness; || תֹּרֶן עַלרֿאֹשׁ הָהָר); signal of war [see SchumacherZPV ix. 232, Across the Jordan, 104 f.] (especially to the nations, for destruction or deliverance of Judah, Isaiah Jeremiah), לַגּוֺיִם ׳ נָשָׂא נ Isa 5:26; Isa 11:12 compare Isa 18:3 (on mountains, || תָּקַע שׁוֺפָר); אֶלעַֿמִּים ׳ אָרִים נ Isa 49:22 (|| אֶשָּׂא אֶלגּֿוֺיִם יָדִי), — in all these subject ׳ י; compare עֹמֵד לְנֵס עַמִּים Isa 10:11 (of Messianic king); ׳ שְׂאוּ נ Isa 13:2 (on mountain), Jer 50:2 ; Jer 51:12 (with אֶלֿ against), Jer 51:27 (|| תִּקְעוּ שׁוֺפָר בַּגּוֺיִם), עַלעַֿמִּים ׳ הָרִימוּ נ Isa 62:10, compare Jer 4:21 (|| קוֺל שׁוֺפָר); לְהִתְנוֺסֵס ׳ נָתַתָּהנ֗֗֗ Ps 60:6 thou hast set a standard (only) for fleeing.
b. signal, צִיּ֫וֺנָה ׳ שְׂאוּ נ Jer 4:6 (i.e. to direct refugees to Zion).
2. standard, as pole, supporting serpent of bronze Num 21:8; Num 21:9 ( J E ).
3.
a. ensign, signal, שֵׁשׁמִ֗֗֗מִּצְרַיִם הָיָה מִפְרָשֵׁךְ לִהְיוֺת לָךְ לְנֵס Ezek 27:7 byssus from Egypt was thy sail, to serve thee for an ensign (so DaToy; on lack of pennon on ancient ships see Co; > strike out Co Berthol); so
b. (since sails were the only ensign) = sail Isa 33:23 (Che Di and others; > pennon Ges Hi Ew De Du).
4. sign (compare Late Hebrew) = warning Num 26:10 ( P ).
